Q: What is the primary purpose of the Global Investment Performance Standards (GIPS)?
A: To guarantee the accuracy of investment returns.
B: To ensure fair representation and full disclosure of investment performance.
C: To prevent investment firms from losing money.
D: To regulate the fees charged by investment managers.
ANSWER: B
EXPLANATION: GIPS standards are voluntary ethical standards based on the principles of fair representation and full disclosure of an investment firm's performance history.

Q: According to the CFA Institute Code of Ethics, members must:
A: Maximize returns for their clients at all costs.
B: Place the integrity of the investment profession and the interests of clients above their own personal interests.
C: Disclose all material nonpublic information to the public immediately.
D: Only invest in socially responsible companies.
ANSWER: B
EXPLANATION: The Code of Ethics explicitly states that members must place the integrity of the investment profession and the interests of clients above their own personal interests.

Q: Which of the following is most likely a violation of the Standard relating to Diligence and Reasonable Basis?
A: Relying on third-party research without verifying the underlying data.
B: Using a quantitative model to make investment decisions.
C: Making an investment recommendation based on a rumor.
D: Failing to keep detailed records of all client communications.
ANSWER: C
EXPLANATION: Making recommendations based on rumors lacks a reasonable and adequate basis, which is a clear violation of the Diligence and Reasonable Basis standard.
